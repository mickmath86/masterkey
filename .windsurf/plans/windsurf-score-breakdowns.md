# Thousand Oaks Area Score Breakdowns — All Areas

## Wildwood (Area Score: 8.8/10)

- **Amenities (7/10):** Wildwood excels in parks, playfields, and trail connectivity (Playfields, Mountclef Ridge access), which anchors daily recreation. The score stops short of 8–9 due to limited walkable retail and dining within the neighborhood itself.
- **Transit & Commute (8/10):** Proximity to Moorpark Rd and fast access to the 101/23 makes commuting efficient by car. Internal streets flow well with low congestion, though the area remains car-dependent.
- **Schools (9/10):** Wildwood Elementary feeds into Redwood Middle and Thousand Oaks High, a pipeline that consistently supports resale demand and long-term value citywide.
- **Economic Stability (9/10):** High owner-occupancy, long tenure, and limited turnover translate into pricing resilience across market cycles.
- **Health & Safety Access (10/10):** Los Robles Hospital, fire stations, and police services are minutes away, offering top-tier emergency response.
- **Crime (10/10):** Exceptionally low violent crime and strong neighborhood security perceptions place Wildwood among the safest pockets in the city.
- **Area Value (8/10):** Entry prices are premium, but buyers receive strong schools, safety, and lifestyle fundamentals that justify cost over time.
- **Space & Density (9/10):** Low density, generous lots, and preserved open space create a quiet, breathable residential environment.

## Conejo Oaks Area (Area Score: 8.6/10)

- **Amenities (8/10):** Close proximity to The Oaks Mall, Janss Marketplace, and regional parks provides strong convenience without intruding on the area's quiet character.
- **Transit & Commute (7/10):** Access to the 101 and 23 is solid, but internal circulation is car-centric and public transit options are limited.
- **Schools (9/10):** CVUSD feeders such as Westlake Hills Elementary, Colina Middle, and Westlake High significantly support family demand.
- **Economic Stability (8/10):** Affluent households and estate-style ownership patterns support consistent pricing and low distress risk.
- **Health & Safety Access (8/10):** Hospitals and emergency services are nearby, though not embedded directly in the neighborhood.
- **Crime (9/10):** Very low crime rates align with the city's safest residential zones.
- **Area Value (6/10):** Lifestyle quality is high, but large-lot premiums reduce relative value efficiency for some buyers.
- **Space & Density (9/10):** Large parcels, minimal through-traffic, and semi-rural spacing define daily life here.

## North Central TO (Area Score: 8.4/10)

- **Amenities (8/10):** Immediate access to trail systems, neighborhood parks, and central Thousand Oaks retail corridors supports a well-rounded lifestyle.

- **Schools (8/10):** Weathersfield Elementary, Redwood Middle, and Thousand Oaks High offer dependable academic outcomes and solid resale support.

## Northeast TO (Area Score: 8.3/10)

- **Amenities (8/10):** Strong access to neighborhood parks, Oakbrook Plaza, and nearby shopping corridors. Daily needs are well covered within a short drive.
- **Transit & Commute (7/10):** Car-dependent but efficient freeway access and most errands require driving.
- **Schools (9/10):** Top-rated schools including Lang Ranch Elementary, Weathersfield Elementary, which consistently support buyer interest and long-term resale strength.
- **Economic Stability (9/10):** Strong owner-occupied base and solid household incomes. Not as affluent as Westlake-adjacent enclaves, it has performed well through market cycles due to broad family appeal.
- **Health & Safety Access (8/10):** Reliable access to Los Robles Hospital, urgent care facilities, and city emergency services within a short drive.
- **Crime (9/10):** Consistently low crime rates and aligns with the safest residential areas in TO.
- **Area Value (8/10):** Buyers receive strong schools, safety, and space relative to much of the city, but pricing has mostly caught up. There are fewer "undervalued" opportunities compared to less-school driven areas.
- **Space & Density (8/10):** Wider streets, larger setbacks, and lower congestion create an open feel. Meaningful separation between neighbors.

## Hillcrest East (Area Score: 8.5/10)

- **Amenities (9/10):** Immediate access to Westlake Village and Thousand Oaks Blvd shopping and dining, and parks makes day-to-day living highly efficient.
- **Transit & Commute (8/10):** Direct freeway proximity and arterial roads support smooth commuting, though public transit remains limited.
- **Schools (8/10):** Conejo Elementary, Colina Middle, and Westlake High provide strong educational anchors.
- **Economic Stability (8/10):** Steady demand driven by Westlake proximity, freeway access, and attracting first-time home buyers and downsizers.
- **Health & Safety Access (8/10):** Hospitals and emergency services are close and easily reachable although not as close to Los Robles Hospital compared to other neighborhoods.
- **Crime (9/10):** Low crime supports strong residential confidence.
- **Area Value (7/10):** HOA-heavy area and HOA costs affect value perception, but convenience offsets the tradeoff.
- **Space & Density (7/10):** Higher-density housing reduces private outdoor space compared to other areas.

## Thousand Oaks South (Area Score: 8.6/10)

- **Amenities (9/10):** Westlake-adjacent shopping, dining, and recreation are immediately accessible.
- **Transit & Commute (9/10):** Excellent freeway access makes this one of the strongest commuter areas in the city.
- **Schools (9/10):** Westlake High and strong CVUSD feeders drive family demand.
- **Economic Stability (9/10):** Strong long-term stability due to high household incomes, consistent owner occupancy, and sustained demand driven by Westlake proximity and commuter convenience.
- **Health & Safety Access (10/10):** Exceptional proximity to hospitals, emergency services, and major roads provides some of the fastest response times and high day-to-day peace of mind in the city.
- **Crime (10/10):** Among the safest zones in Thousand Oaks.
- **Area Value (8/10):** Value is rooted in location and commute efficiency rather than space, making pricing feel justified even if it isn't a price/sf bargain.
- **Space & Density (8/10):** A balanced mix of housing types delivers flexibility and livability without excessive congestion, though density is higher than hillside or large-lot areas.

## Sunset Hills / Copperwood (Area Score: 7.6/10)

- **Amenities (5/10):** Strong outdoor and trail access, but most shopping and dining require a comparatively longer drive.
- **Transit & Commute (3/10):** Highly car-dependent with minimal transit infrastructure.
- **Schools (9/10):** CVUSD schools are a major draw and value anchor.
- **Economic Stability (8/10):** Limited turnover, which supports pricing stability across market cycles. Demand is more lifestyle and family driven than employment-hub-driven, keeping this below the highest tier.
- **Health & Safety Access (8/10):** The hillside layout can add a few minutes to emergency services compared to more central areas. Overall access is strong and dependable.
- **Crime (9/10):** Very low crime environment.
- **Area Value (7/10):** Above average space and privacy, making this area competitive when compared to Westlake-adjacent areas. Value is strongest for buyers prioritizing space rather than walkability or convenience.
- **Space & Density (9/10):** Large lots and hillside spacing define the lifestyle. Minimal traffic. More open and less congested than most central Thousand Oaks neighborhoods.

## Shadow Oaks / Eichler (Area Score: 7.9/10)

- **Amenities (7/10):** Shadow Oaks benefits from a central Thousand Oaks location with convenient access to shopping, dining, and daily services, though it lacks destination-style amenities or walkable commercial hubs within the neighborhood itself.
- **Transit & Commute (6/10):** Freeway access is reasonable but not immediate, and daily life is largely car-dependent. Commute efficiency is adequate, though less streamlined than areas closer to the 101 or major arterial corridors.
- **Schools (5/10):** Local schools are average relative to other Thousand Oaks areas and are not the primary driver of demand here. Buyers drawn to Shadow Oaks typically prioritize architecture and neighborhood character over school rankings.
- **Economic Stability (8/10):** Strong owner occupancy and sustained demand (especially from a niche buyer pool that values Eichler architecture and mid-century design). While not school-driven, this architectural scarcity has historically insulated the area from sharper market swings.
- **Health & Safety Access (8/10):** The area enjoys solid proximity to Los Robles Hospital, urgent care facilities, and city emergency services, providing dependable response times and day-to-day peace of mind.
- **Crime (9/10):** Crime rates are very low and consistent with the safest residential pockets of Thousand Oaks, reinforced by stable ownership and quiet residential streets.
- **Area Value (7/10):** Value in Shadow Oaks is tied more to architectural uniqueness and neighborhood character than to schools or space. Buyers may pay a premium for design pedigree, but long-term desirability has supported stable appreciation.
- **Space & Density (8/10):** Lots are moderate in size with mature landscaping and thoughtful spacing, creating a comfortable, low-congestion environment without the expansiveness of hillside or semi-rural areas.

## Kevington / New Meadows (Area Score: 8.6/10)

- **Amenities (8/10):** Kevington offers close proximity to Westlake shopping, dining, and country clubs, but little within walking distance. Convenience is high by car, not on foot.
- **Transit & Commute (6/10):** Hillside layout requires driving.
- **Schools (10/10):** Elite CVUSD feeder schools are a primary driver of demand and pricing (Westlake High, Colina Middle, Westlake Hills Elementary). School quality is one of the strongest long-term value anchors in the area.
- **Economic Stability (9/10):** Affluent ownership, long tenure, and low turnover create strong resilience through market cycles. Demand is narrower but highly reliable.
- **Health & Safety Access (9/10):** Excellent emergency coverage and safety and well-covered fire and police services.
- **Crime (10/10):** Among the safest areas in the Conejo Valley. Low traffic volume and stable, owner-occupied profile further enhance day-to-day safety and peace of mind.
- **Area Value (6/10):** Pricing reflects schools, safety, and prestige more than space efficiency or convenience. Value is strongest for buyers prioritizing certainty over cost efficiency.
- **Space & Density (9/10):** Large homes on generous lots provide privacy and separation between neighbors. Hillside layouts can reduce walkability and flat yard usability in some pockets.

## Lynn Ranch (Area Score: 7.9/10)

- **Amenities (7/10):** Regional shopping is nearby by car, with trails and equestrian lifestyle serving as the primary on-site amenities.
- **Transit & Commute (6/10):** Car-only lifestyle with efficient freeway access.
- **Schools (7/10):** Solid public schools with private options nearby.
- **Economic Stability (9/10):** High-income, long-term ownership and lifestyle-driven demand support strong stability across market cycles.
- **Health & Safety Access (8/10):** Strong emergency services coverage.
- **Crime (9/10):** Very low crime rates.
- **Area Value (7/10):** Value is strong for those prioritizing space and rural-suburban lifestyle, but less efficient on price/sqft compared to other areas.
- **Space & Density (9/10):** Large parcels, equestrian zoning, and minimal through-traffic create one of the lowest-density environments in Thousand Oaks.
